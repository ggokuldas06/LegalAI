MASTER PROFESSIONAL SERVICES & TECHNOLOGY INTEGRATION AGREEMENT
Effective Date: January 10, 2024

This Master Professional Services & Technology Integration Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into between:

Client: Horizon Biomedical Research Institute (“Client”)
Service Provider: Vertex Systems Integrations, LLC (“Service Provider”)

The Parties agree as follows:

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1 — DEFINITIONS
------------------------------------------------------------
1.1 “Affiliate” means any entity that controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with a Party.

1.2 “Authorized Personnel” means employees, contractors, or agents who have executed confidentiality agreements consistent with this Agreement.

1.3 “Background Technology” includes pre-existing software, templates, tools, libraries, documentation, methods, and processes owned or controlled by Service Provider prior to the Effective Date.

1.4 “Client Materials” means data, research outputs, specifications, datasets, logs, device telemetry, and any other information provided by Client.

1.5 “Compliance Standards” refers to HIPAA, NIST SP 800-53, ISO 27001, and any additional applicable laws or industry standards.

1.6 “Deliverables” include work product, documentation, integration connectors, software modules, dashboards, data pipelines, testing results, and prototypes produced under this Agreement.

1.7 “Milestones” means the project checkpoints as defined in Exhibit B.

1.8 “Project Change Request (PCR)” means a written request to modify scope, cost, or timeline.

1.9 “Security Incident” means any unauthorized disclosure, access, alteration, or misuse of Client Materials.

1.10 “Services” means consulting, development, engineering, testing, documentation, project management, and support provided by Service Provider.

1.11 “Work Product” means anything created by Service Provider during the performance of Services.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2 — SCOPE OF SERVICES
------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 Service Provider shall provide:
(a) data ingestion pipeline development;
(b) secure biomedical device integration;
(c) real-time anomaly detection setup;
(d) cloud-to-local replication architecture;
(e) compliance-aligned documentation;
(f) monitoring dashboards;
(g) automated workflow systems;
(h) API development and integration.

2.2 Project Phases:
Phase I – Architecture & Planning (6 weeks)
Phase II – Development & Integration (14 weeks)
Phase III – Validation, Testing & Knowledge Transfer (10 weeks)
Phase IV – Deployment Support & Stabilization (6 weeks)
Phase V – Optional Long-Term Maintenance (annual renewal)

2.3 Weekly progress meetings with Client’s Technical Director are required.

2.4 Service Provider shall maintain detailed engineering logs, design drafts, Git commit history, and deployment notes.

2.5 Any additional features shall require a PCR.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3 — PAYMENT TERMS
------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Fees:
$250/hour software engineering
$300/hour compliance consulting
$200/hour QA and testing
$175/hour documentation drafting

3.2 Invoices issued monthly with detailed time logs, task breakdowns, and descriptions.

3.3 Late Payment: Any amount not paid within 30 days incurs 1.25% monthly interest.

3.4 Expenses reimbursable with receipts: travel, lodging, specialized equipment, temporary lab access fees.

3.5 Client may request budget forecasts, burn-rate summaries, and projected completion-cost models.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4 — CONFIDENTIALITY
------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 Each Party will protect Confidential Information with the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information.

4.2 Confidentiality obligations last 5 years after termination.

4.3 Forbidden activities include:
(a) disclosure of research data to third parties;
(b) reverse engineering or decoding proprietary systems;
(c) transmitting or storing Client data in unapproved cloud environments;
(d) retaining Client datasets post-termination.

4.4 Confidential Information may only be shared with Authorized Personnel on a need-to-know basis.

4.5 Service Provider shall maintain logs of all personnel with access to Client Materials.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 Client owns all Deliverables and Work Product created during the engagement.

5.2 Service Provider retains full ownership of Background Technology.

5.3 Service Provider grants Client a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free license to use Background Technology only as embedded in Deliverables.

5.4 Service Provider must disclose all open-source components and confirm compliance with license obligations.

5.5 Jointly developed IP will be owned jointly unless otherwise specified.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6 — DATA SECURITY & COMPLIANCE
------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 Systems and Services must comply with:
HIPAA
NIST SP 800-53
ISO 27001
Client’s internal security protocols

6.2 Service Provider shall implement:
network segmentation
encryption in-transit and at-rest
access control with MFA
audit logging
quarterly penetration testing
endpoint monitoring
secure key management

6.3 Any Security Incident must be reported within 24 hours, including:
description of the incident
data affected
containment measures
remediation plan

6.4 Service Provider shall maintain incident logs for a minimum of 7 years.

6.5 Data retention policies must be documented and approved by Client.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 7 — WARRANTIES
------------------------------------------------------------
7.1 Service Provider warrants that:
(a) Deliverables meet specifications in Exhibit A;
(b) work will be performed professionally and in accordance with industry standards;
(c) no malicious code or harmful software will be introduced;
(d) Deliverables will not knowingly infringe third-party IP.

7.2 Disclaimer: Except as explicitly stated, all Deliverables are provided “AS-IS.”

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 8 — INDEMNIFICATION
------------------------------------------------------------
8.1 Service Provider shall indemnify Client for:
(a) IP infringement claims arising from Deliverables;
(b) data breaches caused by Service Provider negligence;
(c) bodily injury or property damage caused by Services;
(d) gross negligence or willful misconduct.

8.2 Client shall indemnify Service Provider for claims arising from Client’s misuse of Deliverables.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 9 — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
------------------------------------------------------------
9.1 Neither Party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages.

9.2 Total liability shall not exceed fees paid by Client in the previous 6 months.

9.3 These limitations do not apply to confidentiality breaches, IP infringement, or data security violations.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 10 — TERMINATION
------------------------------------------------------------
10.1 Either Party may terminate with 30 days’ written notice.

10.2 Immediate termination permitted for:
material breach
fraud or misconduct
violation of data security requirements

10.3 Upon termination, Client must pay for all Services performed to date.

10.4 Service Provider must return or destroy Client Materials within 10 days.

10.5 Final documentation, code, and artifacts must be delivered within 15 days post-termination.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 11 — GOVERNING LAW
------------------------------------------------------------
11.1 This Agreement is governed by California law.

11.2 Exclusive venue is San Mateo County, California.

------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 12 — MISCELLANEOUS
------------------------------------------------------------
12.1 Assignment requires prior written consent.

12.2 Amendments must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

12.3 This Agreement supersedes all prior agreements relating to the subject matter.

12.4 If any provision is invalid, the remaining provisions remain in effect.

12.5 Neither Party shall be considered in breach to the extent performance is delayed due to force majeure events, including natural disasters, power failures, pandemics, war, sanctions, or supply-chain collapse.
