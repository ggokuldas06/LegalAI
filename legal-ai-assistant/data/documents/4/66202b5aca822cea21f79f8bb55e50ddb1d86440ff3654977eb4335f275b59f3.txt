PART VI – TECHNICAL CONTROLS FOR HIGH-SENSITIVITY DATA  

Section 31 — Access Control  
31.1 Organizations must implement multi-factor authentication for all privileged accounts.  
31.2 Access entitlements must be reviewed bi-monthly.  
31.3 Shared administrative accounts are prohibited.  
31.4 Emergency access procedures must be documented.

Section 32 — Encryption  
32.1 All transmissions involving high-sensitivity data must use TLS 1.3 or higher.  
32.2 Encryption keys must be rotated every 180 days.  
32.3 Key material shall be stored in hardware security modules.

Section 33 — Logging & Monitoring  
33.1 Organizations must log all data access, deletions, and permission changes.  
33.2 Logs must include timestamps, actor identity, and event type.  
33.3 Logs must be retained for **36 months**.  
33.4 Continuous monitoring systems must generate alerts for anomalous behavior.

Section 34 — Incident Response  
34.1 Organizations must maintain a 24×7 incident response capability.  
34.2 Incidents must be triaged into Severity Levels 1–5.  
34.3 Level 1–3 incidents must be reported to the governing authority within **72 hours**.  
34.4 Annual simulation exercises are mandatory.

Section 35 — Data Minimization  
35.1 Collection of personal data must be strictly limited to operational necessity.  
35.2 Archived records older than 48 months must undergo mandatory review.  
35.3 Non-essential datasets must be anonymized or deleted.
